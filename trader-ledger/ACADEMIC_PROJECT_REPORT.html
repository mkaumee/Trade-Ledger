ABSTRACT

In multi-supplier retail and wholesale businesses, poor tracking of part-payments and supplier debts causes inaccurate profit calculation and exposes business owners to hidden losses and unrecognized debt. This project presents a comprehensive web-based Profit and Debt Tracking System designed to address these critical challenges faced by small to medium-sized businesses.

The system provides real-time tracking of sales transactions, purchase records, customer debts (accounts receivable), and supplier debts (accounts payable). Built using modern web technologies including Node.js, Express.js, SQLite3, and vanilla JavaScript, the application offers an intuitive interface with interactive data visualizations powered by Chart.js.

Key features include user authentication with bcrypt password hashing, support for partial payment tracking, automated debt aggregation, email notification system for payment reminders, and a comprehensive dashboard with real-time analytics. The system calculates accurate net profit by tracking both collected revenue and paid expenses, while maintaining clear visibility of all outstanding obligations.

The implementation follows a three-tier architecture with a presentation layer (HTML/CSS/JavaScript), application layer (Express.js REST API), and data layer (SQLite3 database). The system is deployed on Railway platform, ensuring accessibility and scalability. Testing results demonstrate the system's effectiveness in preventing financial discrepancies and providing business owners with accurate, real-time financial insights.

This project successfully addresses the problem statement by eliminating manual tracking errors, providing instant visibility into financial obligations, and enabling informed business decisions through accurate profit calculations.

CHAPTER 1
INTRODUCTION

1.1 Background

In the contemporary business landscape, small to medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) in the retail and wholesale sectors face significant challenges in managing their financial operations. These businesses typically engage with multiple suppliers and customers simultaneously, creating complex networks of financial transactions that require meticulous tracking and management. The traditional methods of manual record-keeping using paper ledgers or basic spreadsheets have proven inadequate for handling the intricacies of modern business operations.

Multi-supplier businesses operate in an environment where cash flow management is critical to survival and growth. These enterprises must simultaneously track money owed to them by customers (accounts receivable) and money they owe to suppliers (accounts payable). The complexity increases exponentially when partial payments are involved, as each transaction may have multiple payment installments spread across different time periods.

The advent of digital technology has revolutionized business operations across various sectors. However, many SMEs, particularly in developing economies, continue to rely on outdated manual systems or expensive enterprise resource planning (ERP) solutions that are beyond their financial reach. This creates a significant gap in the market for affordable, user-friendly, and effective financial tracking solutions tailored to the specific needs of multi-supplier businesses.

Research has shown that poor financial management is one of the leading causes of business failure among SMEs. According to various studies, businesses that lack proper tracking mechanisms for their receivables and payables often experience cash flow problems, leading to operational difficulties and, in severe cases, business closure. The inability to accurately calculate profit margins due to untracked debts and partial payments results in flawed business decisions and strategic planning errors.

This project addresses these challenges by developing a comprehensive web-based Profit and Debt Tracking System specifically designed for multi-supplier businesses. The system leverages modern web technologies to provide real-time tracking, automated calculations, and intuitive visualizations that enable business owners to make informed decisions based on accurate financial data.

1.2 Problem Statement

In multi-supplier retail and wholesale businesses, poor tracking of part-payments and supplier debts causes inaccurate profit calculation and exposes business owners to hidden losses and unrecognized debt.

This problem manifests in several critical ways:

  1. Inaccurate Profit Calculation: Business owners often calculate profit based on total sales without accounting for uncollected receivables. This leads to an inflated perception of profitability, as the business may show high sales figures while having significant amounts of money tied up in customer debts. Similarly, unpaid supplier debts may not be properly factored into expense calculations, further distorting the true financial picture.
  2. Hidden Losses: When partial payments are not systematically tracked, businesses lose visibility into the actual cash position. A business might appear profitable on paper while experiencing severe cash flow problems due to uncollected debts. These hidden losses only become apparent when suppliers demand payment or when the business attempts to make significant purchases or investments.
  3. Unrecognized Debt: Without proper tracking mechanisms, supplier debts can accumulate unnoticed until they reach critical levels. This can damage supplier relationships, lead to supply chain disruptions, and in extreme cases, result in legal action. The lack of systematic debt tracking also makes it difficult to prioritize payments and manage working capital effectively.
  4. Manual Tracking Errors: Traditional manual methods are prone to human error, including data entry mistakes, calculation errors, and omissions. These errors compound over time, making it increasingly difficult to reconcile accounts and determine the true financial status of the business.
  5. Lack of Real-Time Visibility: Manual systems do not provide real-time insights into financial status. Business owners must spend considerable time compiling and analyzing data before they can understand their current financial position, by which time the information may already be outdated.
  6. Difficulty in Decision Making: Without accurate and timely financial information, business owners struggle to make informed decisions about pricing, purchasing, credit terms, and business expansion. This can lead to missed opportunities or, conversely, overextension of resources.

The cumulative effect of these issues is that business owners operate with incomplete or inaccurate financial information, leading to poor strategic decisions, strained supplier relationships, cash flow crises, and ultimately, business failure. There is a clear and urgent need for a systematic, automated solution that can accurately track all financial transactions, including partial payments, and provide real-time visibility into the true financial health of the business.

1.3 Objectives

The primary objective of this project is to develop a comprehensive web-based Profit and Debt Tracking System that addresses the identified problems in multi-supplier business financial management. The specific objectives are:

1.3.1 Primary Objectives

  1. Develop an Accurate Profit Calculation System: Create a system that calculates net profit by considering both collected revenue from customers and actual payments made to suppliers, rather than relying solely on gross sales figures. This ensures that business owners have a realistic view of their profitability.
  2. Implement Comprehensive Debt Tracking: Design and implement mechanisms to track both customer debts (accounts receivable) and supplier debts (accounts payable) with complete visibility into outstanding balances, payment histories, and aging analysis.
  3. Support Partial Payment Management: Enable the system to handle partial payments for both sales and purchases, maintaining accurate records of amounts paid, balances remaining, and payment status for each transaction.
  4. Provide Real-Time Financial Visibility: Develop a dashboard that displays real-time financial metrics, including total sales, total purchases, net profit, outstanding receivables, and outstanding payables, enabling instant access to critical financial information.

1.3.2 Secondary Objectives

  1. Implement Secure User Authentication: Develop a robust authentication system that ensures data security and privacy, allowing multiple businesses to use the system while maintaining complete data isolation.
  2. Create Intuitive Data Visualizations: Implement interactive charts and graphs that present financial data in an easily understandable format, facilitating quick comprehension of business performance trends.
  3. Develop Automated Notification System: Create functionality to send payment reminders to customers with outstanding debts, improving collection rates and reducing the time spent on manual follow-ups.
  4. Ensure System Scalability and Accessibility: Deploy the system on a cloud platform to ensure accessibility from any location and device, while maintaining the ability to scale as business needs grow.
  5. Maintain Data Integrity and Persistence: Implement a reliable database system that ensures all financial data is accurately stored, easily retrievable, and protected against loss.
  6. Design Responsive User Interface: Create a user-friendly interface that works seamlessly across desktop, tablet, and mobile devices, ensuring accessibility for business owners regardless of their technical expertise or device preference.

1.4 Scope and Limitations

1.4.1 Scope

The scope of this project encompasses the following:

  • User Management: Registration and authentication system for business owners, with secure password storage and session management.
  • Sales Management: Complete tracking of sales transactions including customer information, item details, quantities, pricing, payment status, and partial payment support.
  • Purchase Management: Comprehensive recording of purchases from suppliers with similar detail levels as sales management.
  • Debt Tracking: Automated aggregation and display of customer debts and supplier debts with the ability to mark debts as paid.
  • Financial Analytics: Real-time calculation and display of key financial metrics including total sales, total purchases, net profit, and outstanding obligations.
  • Data Visualization: Interactive charts showing sales vs. purchases, payment status distribution, and cash flow trends.
  • Email Notifications: System to send payment reminder emails to customers with outstanding balances.
  • Responsive Design: User interface that adapts to different screen sizes and devices.
  • Cloud Deployment: Hosting on Railway platform for accessibility and reliability.

1.4.2 Limitations

The following limitations are acknowledged in the current implementation:

  • Single Currency Support: The system currently supports only Nigerian Naira (₦). Multi-currency transactions are not supported in this version.
  • No Inventory Management: The system does not track inventory levels or provide stock management features.
  • Limited Reporting: Advanced reporting features such as PDF export, custom date ranges, and detailed financial statements are not included.
  • No Multi-User Roles: The system does not support multiple user roles (admin, manager, staff) within a single business account.
  • Basic Email Integration: Email notifications rely on client-side email clients rather than server-side SMTP integration.
  • No Payment Gateway Integration: The system does not integrate with online payment processors for direct payment collection.
  • SQLite Database: The use of SQLite limits concurrent user capacity compared to enterprise database systems like PostgreSQL or MySQL.
  • No Offline Functionality: The system requires an internet connection to function and does not support offline data entry.

LITERATURE REVIEW

2.1 Existing Systems

A comprehensive review of existing financial management systems reveals various solutions available in the market, each with distinct features, advantages, and limitations.

2.1.1 QuickBooks

QuickBooks, developed by Intuit, is one of the most widely used accounting software solutions for small businesses. It offers comprehensive features including invoicing, expense tracking, payroll management, and financial reporting. The system provides robust accounts receivable and accounts payable management with automated reminders and payment tracking.

Advantages: Comprehensive feature set, strong reporting capabilities, integration with banking systems, and extensive third-party integrations.

Limitations: High subscription costs (starting from $25-$180 per month), steep learning curve for non-accountants, and complexity that may be overwhelming for small businesses with simple needs.

2.1.2 Zoho Books

Zoho Books is a cloud-based accounting software designed for small businesses. It provides features for managing sales, purchases, banking, and generating financial reports. The system includes automated workflows and supports multiple currencies.

Advantages: Affordable pricing, user-friendly interface, good integration with other Zoho products, and mobile app availability.

Limitations: Limited customization options, requires internet connection, and some advanced features require higher-tier subscriptions.

2.1.3 Wave Accounting

Wave is a free accounting software targeted at very small businesses and freelancers. It offers basic accounting features including invoicing, receipt scanning, and financial reporting.

Advantages: Free for basic features, simple interface, and suitable for businesses with minimal accounting needs.

Limitations: Limited features compared to paid alternatives, no inventory management, and customer support is limited for free users.

2.1.4 Microsoft Excel/Google Sheets

Many small businesses continue to use spreadsheet software for financial tracking. These tools offer flexibility and customization but require manual data entry and formula creation.

Advantages: Low cost, high flexibility, and familiar interface for most users.

Limitations: Prone to human error, no automated calculations, limited collaboration features, no built-in security, and time-consuming manual processes.

2.1.5 Traditional Paper Ledgers

Some businesses, particularly in developing regions, still rely on paper-based record-keeping systems.

Advantages: No technology requirements, no subscription costs, and complete control over data.

Limitations: Highly prone to errors, no automated calculations, difficult to generate reports, risk of physical damage or loss, and time-consuming to maintain.

2.2 Technology Review

2.2.1 Backend Technologies

Node.js

Node.js is a JavaScript runtime built on Chrome's V8 engine that enables server-side JavaScript execution. It uses an event-driven, non-blocking I/O model that makes it lightweight and efficient for data-intensive real-time applications.

Advantages: Single language (JavaScript) for both frontend and backend, large ecosystem of packages via npm, excellent performance for I/O operations, and strong community support.

Use in Project: Node.js serves as the runtime environment for the backend server, handling all API requests and database operations.

Express.js

Express.js is a minimal and flexible Node.js web application framework that provides a robust set of features for web and mobile applications. It simplifies the process of building RESTful APIs and handling HTTP requests.

Advantages: Lightweight, unopinionated design, extensive middleware ecosystem, and easy routing configuration.

Use in Project: Express.js is used to create the REST API, handle routing, implement middleware for CORS and JSON parsing, and serve static files.

SQLite3

SQLite is a C-language library that implements a small, fast, self-contained, high-reliability, full-featured SQL database engine. Unlike most SQL databases, SQLite does not require a separate server process.

Advantages: Zero configuration, serverless architecture, single file database, ACID compliant, and suitable for embedded applications.

Use in Project: SQLite3 serves as the database management system, storing all user data, sales records, purchase records, and maintaining data integrity through foreign key constraints.

bcryptjs

bcryptjs is a JavaScript implementation of the bcrypt password hashing function. It uses a salt to protect against rainbow table attacks and includes a cost factor to make brute-force attacks computationally expensive.

Advantages: Industry-standard security, adjustable computational cost, and protection against timing attacks.

Use in Project: bcryptjs is used to hash user passwords before storage and to verify passwords during authentication.

2.2.2 Frontend Technologies

HTML5

HTML5 is the latest version of the Hypertext Markup Language, providing semantic elements, improved form controls, and multimedia support without requiring plugins.

Use in Project: HTML5 provides the structural foundation for all user interfaces, including semantic elements for better accessibility and SEO.

CSS3

CSS3 is the latest evolution of Cascading Style Sheets, introducing features like gradients, transitions, animations, flexbox, and grid layouts.

Use in Project: CSS3 is used extensively for styling, creating responsive layouts with flexbox and grid, implementing animations, and ensuring visual consistency across the application.

Vanilla JavaScript (ES6+)

Modern JavaScript (ECMAScript 2015 and later) provides features like arrow functions, promises, async/await, template literals, and destructuring.

Advantages: No framework overhead, faster load times, direct DOM manipulation, and full control over application behavior.

Use in Project: JavaScript handles all client-side logic including form validation, API communication, DOM manipulation, and chart rendering.

Chart.js

Chart.js is an open-source JavaScript library for creating responsive, animated charts. It supports eight chart types and is highly customizable.

Advantages: Easy to use, responsive by default, good documentation, and supports animations.

Use in Project: Chart.js powers the dashboard visualizations including bar charts for sales vs. purchases, doughnut charts for payment status, and line charts for cash flow trends.

Font Awesome

Font Awesome is a font and icon toolkit providing scalable vector icons that can be customized with CSS.

Use in Project: Font Awesome icons enhance the user interface, providing visual cues for navigation, actions, and status indicators.

2.2.3 Deployment Technologies

Railway

Railway is a modern platform-as-a-service (PaaS) that simplifies application deployment with automatic builds, HTTPS support, and environment variable management.

Advantages: Simple deployment process, automatic HTTPS, built-in monitoring, and generous free tier.

Use in Project: Railway hosts the application, providing automatic deployments from GitHub, environment variable management, and persistent storage for the SQLite database.

Nixpacks

Nixpacks is Railway's build system that automatically detects the application type and configures the build process accordingly.

Use in Project: Nixpacks handles the build process, installing Node.js, npm dependencies, and configuring the start command.

  • Capturing user input
  • Validating form data
  • Making API requests to the application layer
  • Displaying data received from the server
  • Rendering charts and visualizations

3.2.2 Application Layer

The application layer is built with Node.js and Express.js, running on the server. This layer handles:

  • RESTful API endpoints
  • Business logic implementation
  • Authentication and authorization
  • Data validation
  • Database operations
  • Error handling

3.2.3 Data Layer

The data layer uses SQLite3 database to provide:

  • Persistent data storage
  • Data integrity through constraints
  • Efficient data retrieval through indexes
  • ACID transaction support

4.2.3 Authentication Implementation

User registration includes password hashing: 

app.post('/api/auth/register', async (req, res) => {
    const { businessName, email, password } = req.body;
    
    // Hash password
    const hashedPassword = await bcrypt.hash(password, 10);
    
    // Insert user
    await runQuery(
        `INSERT INTO users (business_name, email, password)
         VALUES (?, ?, ?)`,
        [businessName, email, hashedPassword]
    );
});

Login verification compares hashed passwords: 

app.post('/api/auth/login', async (req, res) => {
    const { email, password } = req.body;
    
    const user = await getQuery(
        'SELECT * FROM users WHERE email = ?', 
        [email]
    );
    
    const validPassword = await bcrypt.compare(
        password, 
        user.password
    );
    
    if (validPassword) {
        delete user.password;
        res.json({ success: true, user });
    }
});

CONCLUSION AND FUTURE WORK

6.1 Conclusion

This project successfully developed a comprehensive web-based Profit and Debt Tracking System that addresses the critical problem of inaccurate profit calculation and poor debt tracking in multi-supplier businesses. The system provides a practical solution to the challenges faced by small to medium-sized enterprises in managing their financial operations.

6.1.1 Achievement of Objectives

All primary objectives were successfully achieved:

  1. Accurate Profit Calculation: The system calculates net profit by considering actual collected revenue and paid expenses, providing business owners with a realistic view of profitability rather than inflated figures based on gross sales.
  2. Comprehensive Debt Tracking: Both customer debts (accounts receivable) and supplier debts (accounts payable) are tracked automatically with complete visibility into outstanding balances and payment histories.
  3. Partial Payment Management: The system successfully handles partial payments for both sales and purchases, maintaining accurate records of amounts paid and balances remaining.
  4. Real-Time Financial Visibility: The dashboard provides instant access to critical financial metrics, enabling business owners to make informed decisions based on current data.

6.1.2 Problem Resolution

The system effectively addresses the problem statement:

  • Inaccurate Profit Calculation: Resolved through automated calculation of net profit based on actual cash flows rather than gross sales figures.
  • Hidden Losses: Eliminated through real-time visibility into all outstanding obligations and actual cash position.
  • Unrecognized Debt: Prevented through automated debt aggregation and clear display of all supplier obligations.
  • Manual Tracking Errors: Eliminated through automated calculations and data validation.
  • Lack of Real-Time Visibility: Resolved through instant dashboard updates and real-time metrics.
  • Difficulty in Decision Making: Addressed through accurate financial data and intuitive visualizations.

6.1.3 Key Contributions

This project makes several important contributions:

  1. Practical Solution: Provides a working system that can be immediately deployed and used by real businesses.
  2. Affordability: Offers a cost-effective alternative to expensive commercial accounting software.

  4. Technology Demonstration: Shows how modern web technologies can be applied to solve real-world business problems.

Appendix B: Database Schema

-- Users Table
CREATE TABLE users (
    id INTEGER PRIMARY KEY AUTOINCREMENT,
    business_name VARCHAR(255) NOT NULL,
    business_type VARCHAR(50) NOT NULL,
    owner_name VARCHAR(255) NOT NULL,
    email VARCHAR(255) UNIQUE NOT NULL,
    phone VARCHAR(50) NOT NULL,
    address TEXT NOT NULL,
    password VARCHAR(255) NOT NULL,
    created_at DATETIME DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP
);

-- Sales Table
CREATE TABLE sales (
    id INTEGER PRIMARY KEY AUTOINCREMENT,
    user_id INTEGER NOT NULL,
    customer_name VARCHAR(255) NOT NULL,
    customer_email VARCHAR(255),
    item VARCHAR(255) NOT NULL,
    quantity DECIMAL(10, 2) NOT NULL,
    unit VARCHAR(50) NOT NULL,
    price_per_unit DECIMAL(10, 2) NOT NULL,
    total DECIMAL(10, 2) NOT NULL,
    amount_paid DECIMAL(10, 2) DEFAULT 0,
    balance DECIMAL(10, 2) DEFAULT 0,
    status VARCHAR(20) NOT NULL DEFAULT 'unpaid',
    sale_date DATE NOT NULL,
    created_at DATETIME DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP,
    FOREIGN KEY (user_id) REFERENCES users(id) ON DELETE CASCADE
);

-- Purchases Table
CREATE TABLE purchases (
    id INTEGER PRIMARY KEY AUTOINCREMENT,
    user_id INTEGER NOT NULL,
    supplier_name VARCHAR(255) NOT NULL,
    item VARCHAR(255) NOT NULL,
    quantity DECIMAL(10, 2) NOT NULL,
    unit VARCHAR(50) NOT NULL,
    price_per_unit DECIMAL(10, 2) NOT NULL,
    total DECIMAL(10, 2) NOT NULL,
    amount_paid DECIMAL(10, 2) DEFAULT 0,
    balance DECIMAL(10, 2) DEFAULT 0,
    status VARCHAR(20) NOT NULL DEFAULT 'unpaid',
    purchase_date DATE NOT NULL,
    created_at DATETIME DEFAULT CURRENT_TIMESTAMP,
    FOREIGN KEY (user_id) REFERENCES users(id) ON DELETE CASCADE
);

-- Indexes
CREATE INDEX idx_sales_user_id ON sales(user_id);
CREATE INDEX idx_sales_status ON sales(status);
CREATE INDEX idx_purchases_user_id ON purchases(user_id);
CREATE INDEX idx_purchases_status ON purchases(status);
CREATE INDEX idx_users_email ON users(email);
